Brain Dump
Privacy Policy
Last updated: June 2026  ·  https://eko-0t2.github.io/brain-dump

Overview

Brain Dump is a personal productivity web application that helps you capture, sort, and manage tasks by integrating with your Google Calendar, Google Tasks, and Google Docs. This privacy policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is stored.

Brain Dump is a personal-use application intended for use by a single individual. It is not a commercial product and is not designed to collect or process data from multiple users.

What Data The App Accesses

When you connect your Google account, the app requests access to the following:

Google Calendar (read-only)

  • Your calendar events for the next 7 days and past 30 days
  • Calendar names, colours, and event details including title, time, location, and description
  • This data is used to display your schedule in the app and provide context to the AI assistant

Google Tasks (read and write)

  • Your existing tasks — titles, due dates, and notes
  • The app creates new tasks, marks tasks complete, updates due dates, and deletes tasks on your behalf
  • This is the core function of the app — converting your brain dump into actionable tasks

Google Docs (read and write)

  • A single Google Doc of your choosing, which you create yourself and specify in Settings
  • The app appends new entries to this document when you save ideas to your idea library
  • The app does not access any other Google Docs

How Your Data Is Used

  • Your Google Calendar data is sent to the Anthropic API (Claude) to provide context for sorting your brain dump
  • Your Google Tasks are summarised and sent to the Anthropic API to avoid creating duplicate tasks
  • No personal data is stored on any server — the app runs entirely in your browser
  • Your API keys and settings are stored only in your browser's local storage on your device
  • Clearing your browser data will remove all stored keys and settings

Third-Party Services

Anthropic API

Brain dump text, calendar summaries, and task summaries are sent to Anthropic's API (api.anthropic.com) for AI processing. This data is subject to Anthropic's privacy policy and terms of service, available at anthropic.com/privacy.

Google APIs

The app uses Google Calendar API, Google Tasks API, and Google Docs API. Your use of these APIs is subject to Google's privacy policy, available at policies.google.com/privacy.

GitHub Pages

The app is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may log standard web server information such as IP address, browser type, and pages visited. This is subject to GitHub's privacy statement at docs.github.com.

Data Retention

  • The app does not retain any of your personal data on a server
  • Data sent to the Anthropic API is subject to Anthropic's retention policies
  • Data written to your Google Tasks and Google Doc remains under your control in your Google account
  • You can revoke the app's access to your Google account at any time at myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps

Data Sharing

Your data is not sold, rented, or shared with any third party other than the services described above — Anthropic API for AI processing, Google APIs for data access, and GitHub Pages for hosting.

Security

  • Your Anthropic API key and Google OAuth credentials are stored only in your browser's local storage
  • All communication with Google APIs and the Anthropic API uses HTTPS encryption
  • The app does not have a backend server — there is no database where your data could be breached

Your Rights

  • You can revoke Google access at any time via your Google account settings
  • You can delete all locally stored app data by clearing your browser's local storage or site data for eko-0t2.github.io
  • You can delete any tasks or doc entries the app has created directly in Google Tasks and Google Docs

Scope Justifications

This app uses the following Google OAuth scopes:

google.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly
Brain Dump reads the user's Google Calendar to display upcoming events in the check-in screen and provide schedule context to the AI assistant when sorting tasks. Calendar data is read-only — the app never creates or modifies calendar events.
google.googleapis.com/auth/tasks
Brain Dump creates new tasks in Google Tasks when the user completes a brain dump session, and updates existing tasks with new due dates or completion status during the check-in flow. Read access is used to surface existing tasks and avoid creating duplicates.
google.googleapis.com/auth/documents
Brain Dump appends ideas to a single Google Doc (an idea library) that the user creates and specifies themselves in Settings. The app only accesses this one document — it does not browse or access any other Google Docs.

Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to This Policy

This privacy policy may be updated when new features are added. The last updated date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

This is a personal-use app. For any questions about this privacy policy, you can reach the developer via the GitHub repository at github.com/eko-0t2/brain-dump.
